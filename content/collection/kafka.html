Kafka on the Shore

Release Date: 2002

Genre: Magical Realism, Surrealism

Description: "Kafka on the Shore" is an enigmatic and spellbinding novel by renowned Japanese author Haruki Murakami. The narrative unfolds with a sense of dreamlike mystery, blurring the boundaries between the real and the surreal. At its center is Kafka Tamura, a fifteen-year-old boy who flees his home to escape a dark prophecy foretold by his father. Alongside Kafka's journey, we encounter Nakata, an elderly man who possesses the ability to converse with cats but struggles with his own fragmented memory. As their paths converge, reality becomes malleable, and the reader is drawn into a labyrinth of symbolism and allegory. Murakami weaves together themes of identity, fate, and the subconscious with masterful prose, inviting readers on a profound exploration of the human psyche. Through its intricate narrative and vivid imagery, "Kafka on the Shore" challenges conventional perceptions of reality and leaves an indelible impression on those who venture into its captivating world.

Main Characters:

  • Kafka Tamura (Male, 15)
  • Nakata (Male, 60+)
  • Oshima (Male, 24)

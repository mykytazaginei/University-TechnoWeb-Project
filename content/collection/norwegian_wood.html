Norwegian Wood

Release Date: 1987

Genre: Coming-of-age, Romance

Description: "Norwegian Wood" is an emotionally charged novel by Japanese author Haruki Murakami, spanning a period of turbulent societal change in late 1960s Japan. At its heart, the story follows the journey of Toru Watanabe, a young man navigating the complexities of love, loss, and identity against the backdrop of a rapidly evolving world. Set amidst the vibrant streets of Tokyo, the narrative delves into the profound connections formed between Toru and two pivotal figures in his life: Naoko, a fragile young woman grappling with her own inner demons, and Midorikawa, a charismatic and enigmatic friend. Murakami's evocative prose transports readers into a realm where themes of melancholy, desire, and the search for meaning intertwine, creating a poignant and unforgettable exploration of the human condition. Through its introspective narrative and richly drawn characters, "Norwegian Wood" captivates readers with its raw emotional honesty and profound insights into the complexities of the human experience.

Main Characters:

  • Toru Watanabe (Male, 19)
  • Naoko (Female, 19)
  • Midorikawa (Male, 20)

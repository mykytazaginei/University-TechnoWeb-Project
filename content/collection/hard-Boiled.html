Hard-Boiled Wonderland and the End of the World

Release Date: 1985 (Original Japanese Edition)

Genre: Science Fiction, Surrealism

Description: "Hard-Boiled Wonderland and the End of the World" is a mind-bending novel by the acclaimed Japanese author Haruki Murakami. The narrative unfolds in parallel worlds: one a futuristic Tokyo where a "Calcutec" agent navigates a dangerous underworld of data encryption, and the other a surreal, dreamlike village devoid of technology. As the two narratives converge, themes of identity, consciousness, and the nature of reality emerge. Murakami's unique blend of hard-boiled detective fiction and speculative fiction creates a mesmerizing journey into the depths of the human psyche. With its intricate plot, rich symbolism, and philosophical undertones, "Hard-Boiled Wonderland and the End of the World" invites readers to explore the boundaries of perception and existence.

Main Characters:

  • The Calcutec (Male)
  • The Librarian (Female)
  • The Professor (Male)
